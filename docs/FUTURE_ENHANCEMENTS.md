# Future Enhancements — Pages & Content Ideas

Ideas to grow traffic and reach beyond the current six-page site, grouped by purpose.

## SEO / evergreen content
- **"Mother Languages of the World" explainer hub** — one of the highest-search-volume topics adjacent to IMLD; ranks well and is genuinely on-mission.
- **Language spotlight series** — short posts profiling one endangered or minority language per month (ties into the blog feature + social media).
- **"History of IMLD" as its own interactive page** — the long-form copy already exists; turning it into a visual timeline (Bill S-214, Bill C-407, Bill S-247 milestones) is shareable and link-worthy for schools and press.
- **Resources for teachers / school districts** — lesson plans, IMLD classroom kits, printable posters. Schools already recognize MLLWS (Surrey, Vancouver, Richmond, Langley, Ottawa Catholic); a resources hub turns that relationship into recurring traffic every February.

## Community & engagement
- **Volunteer / Get Involved page** with a simple sign-up form (Zeffy also supports volunteer/RSVP forms, or a lightweight Google Form embed).
- **Newsletter sign-up** (Mailchimp/Buttondown embed) — currently there's no way to capture repeat visitors.
- **Community stories / testimonials** — short submissions from festival attendees, chapter members, students about what their mother language means to them. User-generated content drives sharing.
- **Photo & video gallery** per festival year — the site currently has almost no visual archive of past festivals; likely easy to source and strong for social proof.

## Reach / press
- **Press & media kit page** — logos, fact sheet, past coverage, and a media contact; makes it easy for journalists to cover the festival or IMLD milestones.
- **Partner / sponsor spotlight page** — expand beyond the logo strip with short write-ups on each sponsor; sponsors often share pages that feature them, which is free reach.
- **Multilingual site toggle** — even a partial translation (Bangla, French per Canadian bilingualism, and 2–3 of the most common Surrey school-district languages) meaningfully widens reach and is on-theme for a mother-language organization. `next-intl` is the standard tool for this in Next.js.

## Interactive
- **Interactive map** of chapters (US, UK) and school districts that recognize IMLD — visually reinforces the "movement" narrative.
- **"Say hello in your mother language" widget** — a fun, shareable micro-interaction (visitor picks a language, sees a greeting + audio clip); good for social media pickup.

## Prioritization note
Highest-leverage first pass, if sequencing is needed: photo/video gallery (uses existing assets), resources-for-teachers hub (leverages existing school relationships), then newsletter sign-up (compounding value — every later launch benefits from having a list already). The multilingual toggle and interactive map are bigger lifts, best scheduled after the blog/MDX pipeline (from [`FEATURES.md`](./FEATURES.md)) is in place.
