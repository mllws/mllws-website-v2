Our story

A handful of neighbours who wanted to hear their languages spoken proudly — grown into a nationwide celebration.

In 2012, a small group of neighbours in Toronto gathered in a community hall to read poems in their mother tongues — Bengali, Tagalog, Somali, Ukrainian — languages rarely heard on a public stage in Canada.

That single evening became an annual festival for International Mother Language Day, then a year-round program of storytelling nights, youth workshops and language circles across the Greater Toronto Area.

Today we're a volunteer-run non-profit powered by hundreds of members, dozens of community partners, and one simple belief: every language deserves to be celebrated, not just preserved.

Milestones

2012
First community poetry reading in a Toronto community hall — 30 people, 4 languages.
2016
Registered as a non-profit; launched weekly Language Circles across three neighbourhoods.
2020
Launched the Youth Ambassadors program to bring younger generations into the celebration.
2026
5,000+ members, 40+ languages celebrated, and our biggest festival yet planned for 2027.

What we believe

Community over ceremony

Every language matters

Widely spoken or nearly gone — every mother tongue gets the same stage.

Kids lead the future

We invest in youth so this outlives any one generation.
